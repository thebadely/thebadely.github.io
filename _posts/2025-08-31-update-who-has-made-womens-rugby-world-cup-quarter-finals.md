Seven teams have booked their places in the  Women's Rugby World Cup quarter-finals after the second round of pool matches.

Canada, Scotland and England qualified on Saturday and Ireland, New Zealand, South Africa and France joined them on Sunday.

Ireland scored seven tries on Sunday to clinch a bonus-point 43-27 win over Spain.

Scott Bemand's side will face defending champions New Zealand in Brighton next Sunday to decide who wins Pool C.

Teenage full-back Braxton Sorensen-McGee scored a hat-trick as the Black Ferns overcame Japan 62-19 to confirm their quarter-final spot. 

South Africa overcame Italy 29-24 in a thrilling contest to qualify for the knockout stage for the first time in their history.

France also progressed following a 14-try 84-5 win over Brazil. The Springbok Women and France meet next week with top spot in Pool D at stake.

Scotland overcame Fiji 29-15 in Salford on Saturday and will face second seeds Canada next weekend to determine the Pool B winners.

Wales' hopes of qualifying for the last eight ended with a 42-0 defeat by Canada.

In Pool A, Jess Breach scored a hat-trick as tournament favourites England scored 14 tries in a record 92-3 World Cup win over Samoa.

Australia and the United States drew a dramatic match 31-31 with both still in contention for the second Pool A qualifying spot.

Teams are awarded four points for a win, two points for a draw and zero points for a loss. 

However, teams will also be awarded a bonus point if they score four tries or more, and/or if they lose by seven points or less.

The top two teams from each pool qualify for the knockout stage.
