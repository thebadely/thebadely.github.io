Highlights: Superb England beat South Africa to set up World Cup final against Australia

England's women have a chance to win their first major title since 2017 as they take on Australia in the T20 World Cup final on Sunday. 

The final starts at 15:30 BST and will be played at Lord's, where England won the 50-over tournament nine years ago. 

Six-time champions Australia brushed aside West Indies in their semi-final, while England cruised past South Africa in theirs as both teams go into the showpiece event unbeaten. 

From ball-by-ball radio commentary to in-play video highlights, BBC Sport has got you covered so that you do not miss any of the action. 

Here is a guide on how to follow the final on the BBC.

There will be ball-by-ball radio commentary from the Test Match Special team on BBC Sounds, BBC Radio 5 Sports Extra and the BBC Sport website and app (UK users only). 

Overseas listeners can tune in via the ICC website. 

You can also listen on your smart speaker, just say "play 5 Sports Extra".

BBC commentators Henry Moeran, Geoff Lemon and Alison Mitchell are joined by World Cup winners Ebony Rainford-Brent, Alex Hartley and Isa Guha. 

In addition, you can also listen worldwide on BBC Hindi with Annesha Ghosh, Shachi Pai and Anjum Chopra.

The BBC Sport website and app will also provide comprehensive coverage of the final.

There will be live text commentary, followed by reaction and analysis shortly after the conclusion. 

The live page on the BBC Sport website and app is also where you will find the in-play video clips of all the key moments, and there will be highlights shortly after the game (UK users only). 

Find out more about the England players with our iPlayer, YouTube and BBC Sounds series "Inside the England camp" featuring Heather Knight, Lauren Bell, Issy Wong, Sophia Dunkley, Freya Kemp, Lauren Filer and Amy Jones. 

There will be a Test Match Special podcast on BBC Sounds after the final.

You can also listen to the reaction to England's emphatic semi-final victory and the team look ahead to how to beat Australia. 

There is also the 2017 reunion podcast, where Isa Guha is joined by members of England's World Cup winning side to look back on that famous day at Lord's.
