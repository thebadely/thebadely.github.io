Let's dive into the details: Bukayo Saka, Alexis Mac Allister and Kobbie Mainoo will return to Arsenal, Liverpool and Manchester United after very different World Cup experiences

Now Spain have been crowned world champions after victory over Argentina in New Jersey, attention will quickly shift to the new domestic season.

The Premier League was well represented in North America, with all 20 of the top-flight teams for 2026-27 having at least one player involved.

From injuries to transfers, here is how the World Cup has impacted your Premier League team.

William Saliba went off during the first half of France's semi-final defeat by Spain

Arsenal may be the Premier League team affected most by the World Cup.

The Gunners face an anxious wait over the fitness of William Saliba, who had been managing a back issue before his tournament was ended by the same problem.

The centre-back has not yet been assessed by the club's medical team, but if he was sidelined, it would be a big loss - especially as his defensive partnership with Gabriel is so crucial to how the team play.

The other two question marks will be over the fitness of Declan Rice and Bukayo Saka, who helped England finish third while managing their own fitness issues.

It does not look like they will need a longer lay-off once the tournament is over, but they will be monitored closely by Arsenal before the new season.

Gabriel Martinelli, who showed flashes of his quality playing for Brazil, is another player the Gunners could be open to selling this summer.

Carlo Ancelotti played the winger in a central role during the tournament, and that may be an extra attraction for potential buyers.

Amadou Onana's knee injury has seriously impacted Aston Villa's preparations

Aston Villa's transfer plans were thrown a curveball by an injury to Amadou Onana.

The midfielder ruptured the anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) in his right knee during Belgium's last-16 win over the USA which will rule him out until next year.

Winger Rayan was a rare bright spot in a disappointing World Cup for Brazil

Bournemouth fans will have been encouraged by the performances of Ben Gannon-Doak at the World Cup, despite Scotland's brief stay in the tournament. The winger played in all three group matches, and particularly impressed in their sole win over Haiti.

The Cherries had six players at the World Cup, including New Zealand back-up goalkeeper Alex Paulsen who has joined Motherwell on loan for the coming season.

Starring among them, as well as Gannon-Doak, was winger Rayan. The 19-year-old started all Brazil's games bar their opener and claimed an assist in the 3-0 win over Scotland to seal their path to the knockout stage.

It further raises Rayan's stock following his January move to Bournemouth, and superb performances in the second half of last season.

This could be bad news for Bournemouth though, with Rayan heavily linked with Liverpool, managed by former Cherries boss Andoni Iraola.

Igor Thiago played only 62 minutes at the World Cup for Brazil, despite starting their opening fixture

For a couple of Brentford players, the World Cup could not have gone much worse.

Striker Igor Thiago came into the tournament on the back of a stellar scoring season for the Bees and tipped as Brazil's lead attacker in North America. However, after a lacklustre performance in the opener against Morocco he was dropped by manager Carlo Ancelotti and did not appear again.

It remains to be seen what impact this has on Thiago's confidence heading into the new season.

Meanwhile, midfielder Jordan Henderson made one substitute appearance for England, but his most memorable contribution was falling over an advertising hoarding during the celebrations following the win over Mexico and requiring surgery on a broken wrist.

Henderson, however, stayed with the England squad and should be fit for the start of Brentford's season, though he may have to initially play in a cast.

Crystal Palace were one of the most represented Premier League clubs at the World Cup, with 10 players involved.

They received a potential blow, however, when Spain winger Yeremy Pino sustained a shoulder injury during the group stage, and missed Spain's victory in the World Cup final.

Iliman Ndiaye scored once and registered two assists for Senegal at the World Cup

Iliman Ndiaye is one of Everton's biggest assets and was at the centre of transfer talk before the World Cup.

All talks on his future were shelved until the end of the World Cup, so a decision on the 36-year-old's next move will be made soon.

Nathan Patterson played for Scotland at right-back, but he has failed to convince manager David Moyes that he should be a regular starter at Everton.

The Toffees are actively pursuing targets in that position, so Patterson could yet be on the move before the start of the season.
