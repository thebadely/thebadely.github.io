Huddersfield Giants legend Leroy Cudjoe is to retire at the end of the season.

The 37-year-old centre has made 406 appearances for the Giants since making his debut in 2008.

Cudjoe will stay with the club in role of transition pathway manager, working with academy players moving up into the first team.

"The Huddersfield Giants, and the whole town of Huddersfield would like to thank Leroy for his outstanding contribution to his hometown club throughout his playing career," the club said in a statement., external

The former England international, who was part of the team that won the League Leaders' Shield in 2013, will play his final home game against Salford on Sunday.
