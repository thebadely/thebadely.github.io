Here’s what you need to know: The government's decision to abandon plans to postpone local elections across 30 councils in England this May is the lead for many of this morning's papers. 

The Mail describes, what it says is Sir Keir Starmer's 14th major U-turn during his time in office, as a "new humiliation" for the prime minister. 

The Telegraph says it's heard from councillors and other local authority insiders who have spoken about the "chaos", "turmoil" and the "unnecessary race against time", they now face.

The i Paper quotes one council leader as saying that local authorities are "experiencing whiplash" from changing government decisions, "making it almost impossible" to plan effectively for residents. It says the U-turn is "triggering" the next threat to Sir Keir's leadership.

The Times describes as a "watershed moment" the fact that GP records can now be linked to the UK "Biobank". It holds the data of half a million middle-aged Britons who signed up to give blood samples to the project, more than twenty years ago. 

The paper says the link hadn't been possible before because of bureaucracy. It quotes researchers as saying that now NHS England has taken over the legal liability for the data, they will be able to use it to understand what's really causing disease and help them solve major health challenges.

The Guardian says that UK bank chiefs will hold a first meeting this week to discuss alternatives to Visa and Mastercard because of fears that President Trump has the ability to turn off US owned payment systems. 

The paper reports that the meeting will bring together a group of city funders, who will cover the cost of a new payments company to keep the UK economy going, in case problems occur. The paper says the initiative, which would be government-backed, has been around for years, but that Donald Trump's recent threats against Greenland have raised concerns.

Many of the papers carry photos of the Oscar-winning actor Robert Duvall whose death was announced yesterday. He was 95 and best known for roles in films such as the Godfather and Apocalypse Now. 

The Telegraph calls him a "scene-stealing star". The Times says he was considered the quintessential "thinking man's tough guy".
