Jess Thirlby has stepped down as England head coach with immediate effect in a decision she says is "best for me and my family".

Thirlby took charge of the Roses after the departure of Tracey Neville in 2019 and guided England to World Cup silver in 2023. 

She leaves the role four months before this summer's Commonwealth Games in Glasgow, which take place from 23 July to 2 August.

Thirlby's assistant Anna Stembridge, who was England head coach between 2011-2015, will lead the side into this summer's Games with former captain Olivia Murphy becoming assistant coach.

With the Commonwealth Games - one of netball's showpiece events - mere months away, the timing of this decision is huge.

Thirlby's statement suggests it has been taken because of off-court matters rather than the Roses' performances, but their preparations for this summer's Games have been rocky.

A change of coach so close to the tournament will only unsettle things further, but England must now focus on finishing on the Commonwealth podium this summer having failed to do so four years ago in what some would cite as the low point of Thirlby's tenure.

She will be fondly remembered for her part in England's historic World Cup run in 2023, when they beat world number one side Australia en route to a maiden final - but fell agonisingly short against them in the showpiece.

Neville was always going to be a tough act to follow and she has often divided opinion, with some suggesting her tactics and team selections have played it too safe when faced with the best in the world.

Former Team Bath head coach Stembridge has experience of coaching England and it is therefore unsurprising that she will take over in the interim - but she faces a difficult challenge to prepare the team so close to a major tournament.
