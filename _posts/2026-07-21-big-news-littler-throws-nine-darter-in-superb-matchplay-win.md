Let's dive into the details: Luke Littler is the defending World Matchplay champion

World number one Luke Littler threw a thrilling nine-darter as he booked his place in the quarter-finals of the World Matchplay with a 11-8 win over Nathan Aspinall. 

Littler began the contest at Winter Gardens in Blackpool in sensational fashion with a perfect leg, finishing on double 15.

The Warrington teenager cupped his ears to lap up the adulation of a delirious crowd while fellow Englishman Aspinall laughed in disbelief before he gave Littler a high five.

Excitement levels were raised further in the fourth leg when Littler had double 15 remaining for a second nine-darter, only for his arrow to land just the wrong side of the wire.

Aspinall turned to the crowd and jokingly offered up his darts to see if anyone else wanted to give the 19-year-old a game. 

He refused to allow Littler to pull away, though, and managed to haul himself back on terms at 7-7 only for the defending champion to reel off the next three legs.

Aspinall briefly rallied again but when he missed tops in the 19th leg, Littler was able to checkout 52 as he sealed victory with an incredible 113.68 average - the second highest in the tournament's history - featuring a total of 14 180s.

"The nine-darter just happened. These guys [the crowd] were booing me at the beginning and then they started cheering me on!" Littler told Sky Sports.

"It felt amazing and that was probably the best game Nathan and I have played against each other - it's never over against Nathan because he never gives up.

"That's two unbelievable performances from myself here. Hopefully I can pick up the title again, but I just have to focus on who I play next."

Littler will face Josh Rock, who impressed in a 11-6 victory over Stephen Bunting. The pair met in last year's Matchplay semi-final.

Elsewhere, Michael van Gerwen's campaign ended as he suffered a 14-12 defeat to fellow Dutchman Dirk van Duijvenbode. 

Van Gerwen had battled back from 8-4 down to level the match at 10-all but Van Duijvenbode closed out the contest.

Scotland's Gary Anderson also progressed to the last eight with an 11-7 win over Welshman Jonny Clayton.
