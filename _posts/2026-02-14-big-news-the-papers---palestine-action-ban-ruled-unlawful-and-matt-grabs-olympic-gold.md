Experts revealed that The Times reports that Thames Valley Police have held "informal discussions" with the government about emails and documents relating to Andrew Mountbatten-Windsor and Jeffrey Epstein. The paper says police are assessing whether there are grounds for an investigation into emails suggesting the former prince leaked confidential information to Epstein. Government sources have told the BBC they are not aware of any discussions but it would not be surprising if they had happened between civil servants and the police. Andrew Mountbatten-Windsor has denied any wrongdoing.  Thames Valley Police have been approached for comment.

The Daily Mail leads on claims that when he was European Union trade commissioner in 2004, Lord Mandelson travelled on a private jet belonging to a Russian oligarch, to meet Russian President Vladimir Putin. They were attending an EU-Russia summit at The Hague. The Conservatives call the revelations disturbing and say they should be investigated. Lord Mandelson tells the paper he cannot remember travel arrangements from so long ago.

The Daily Telegraph leads on an interview with the chief executive of the Electoral Commission. Vijay Rangarajan criticises ministers for allowing 30 local authorities to postpone elections due in May. Ministers have argued, the paper notes, that councils cannot afford to both hold elections and prepare for a re-organisation of local government. Rangarajan tells the Telegraph that constraints in capacity are not a sufficient reason to take away from voters the chance to choose who represents them.

Heathrow Airport feels so crowded because passengers keep bumping into each other, its chief executive, Thomas Woldbye, is reported as saying in the Financial Times. He says British people keep to the left and Europeans to the right - so they crash into each other and the terminals then feel busier than they actually are.
