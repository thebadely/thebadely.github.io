Experts revealed that President Donald Trump's threat of legal action against the BBC features on most of the front pages, including the Sun which describes Panorama's edit of his speech as the "Beeb's Billion Dollar Bungle".

"Trump Tells BBC: Grovel - Or I'll Sue", says the Daily Mail's headline.

The Daily Telegraph says the "extraordinary" move by the US president has "turned the screw" on a corporation that's "reeling" from the resignations of the director general and the head of news.

While the i Paper says the future of the licence fee is now in jeopardy, with the BBC "left weakened" ahead of negotiations with the government.

The Guardian claims a BBC board member with links to the Conservatives "led the charge" in pressuring the corporation's leadership over claims of systemic bias, before Tim Davie and Deborah Turness announced they were stepping down.

Sources have told the paper that Theresa May's former director of communications, Sir Robbie Gibb, "amplified the criticism" in key meetings.  Writing in the Guardian, Liberal Democrat leader Sir Ed Davey says Sir Robbie should be removed from the BBC board before the search begins for a new director general.
